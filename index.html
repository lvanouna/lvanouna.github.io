Lucas van Oudheusden Navarro

Master’s student in Pure and Applied Logic (Universitat de Barcelona)
Logic, proof theory, modal logic, cryptography — and audio/DSP on the side.

I’m a Master’s student in Pure and Applied Logic at the Universitat de Barcelona, with a background in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics. My academic interests sit around proof theory, modal logic, model theory, and the ways formal systems can be made more intuitive and usable.

I’m currently writing a thesis (W.I.P., intended for publication) on admissibility rules of Peano Arithmetic using provability logic GL. Alongside this, I run voluntary tutoring sessions in mathematical logic for undergraduates, focusing on proofs, semantics, and model-theoretic reasoning.

Outside of logic, I’m deeply into music and audio: listening, collecting records, and increasingly the technical side — DSP, JUCE, and building tools that make sound feel sharp and intentional.

Music and Audio

I care about music in two ways: as a listener/collector, and as a technical rabbit hole. Right now I’m putting serious attention into DSP and JUCE, aiming to build audio tools and effects that feel musical first and “engineering-y” second.

DSP & JUCE

Interests: filters & EQ, dynamics, distortion/saturation, time-based effects, and the psychoacoustics of “why this sounds good”. I’m learning JUCE to turn DSP ideas into plugins and small audio apps.

Listening & collecting

I track listening on Last.fm, collect records on Discogs, and use RateYourMusic for digging and cataloguing. If you’re into electronic, experimental, dub, metal, or left-field pop, we’ll probably get on.
